Showing that Electric Vehicles with No Engine Cylinders have the Highest MPG(e)

Until early 2000s, almost all vehicles ran on gasoline and diesel
With the invention of hybrid and electric vehicles, it has become necessary to compare the energy efficiency of electric, gasoline, and diesel vehicles
In this visualization, we will compare the energy efficiency of electric, diesel, and gas vehicles
The energy efficiency for gas and diesel vehicles is measured in Miles Per Gallon(MPG).
The energy efficiency for electric vehicles is measured in Miles Per Gallon Equaivalent(MPGe) since it uses battery rather than fuel.
MPGe is the distance an electric vehicle can travel on 33kWh of electricity which if equivalent to 1 gallon of gas fuel
Engine cylinders are chambers where fuel is combusted and power is generated for gasoline and diesel vehicles. Electric vehicles have no engine cylinders since they use a battery
For the purposes of this presentation we will be using the unit MPG(e) to for energy efficiency of electric and tradition fuel
